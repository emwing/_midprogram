Fall 2016 Web Design Final

Independently redesign the PacSet Tours company website.

Project Background

In the fall of 2015 I enlisted the services of PacSet Tours to manage my first-ever trip to Japan. They were amazing, the other tour participants were fantastic, and the trip was just about perfect. I befriended everyone in the tour group including the tour guide who also happened to be the company's CEO.

When I learned that the Web Design class' final project could be a site redesign, I immediately thought of PacSet's site and reached out to the CEO. By coincidence, he had just started working with a designer to redesign the site, but he happily granted me educational access to the site's assets and chatted with me about some of his hopes and expectations for the new design. With his blessing, I began the eight-week process of independently redesigning the website.

My Role

Research, conceptualize, design, and develop the website with limited interaction and direction from the client.

Project Goals

  • Responsive layout
  • Refresh branding
  • Clarify visual hierarchy
  • Streamline User Experience
  • Develop page templates for individual tour pages
  • Consolidate information

Technologies

  • HTML5
  • CSS3
  • Instructor-provided JavaScript
  • Photoshop CC
  • Illustrator CC
  • InDesign CC

Idea Board

I developed this Idea Board to show how existing brand colors could be expanded for a deeper pallete, the preferred vector-style icons could be leveraged in more site elements to distinguish the brand, and maintain an emphasis on showcasing inviting imagery.

Redesigned Site Map

The original site had many pages of information that were overwhelming for users to read and navigate. After reviewing the content, I found many pages could be consolidated or categorized together. This new sitemap shows the reduction of the top-level page count, which helps prevent information-overload.
